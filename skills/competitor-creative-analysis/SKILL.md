# Competitor Creative Analysis — Meta

## What it does
Pulls competitor ads from Meta Ad Library, categorizes their messaging angles, formats, CTAs, and creative types, then identifies gaps in their approach you can exploit and patterns worth testing in your own campaigns.

## How it works
Review competitor ad libraries and break down what they're running — how many active ads, what formats (video, static, carousel), what messaging themes (pain point, benefit, social proof, urgency), how often they rotate, and what offers they're pushing. Map this against your current creative strategy to find differentiation opportunities.

## What you need from the user
- Competitor brand names or Meta Ad Library URLs
- Your own current creative angles and formats (to identify differentiation)
- Product category and target audience
- Time window to focus on (recent launches, past 30/90 days)

## How to pull data
1. Go to Meta Ad Library: https://www.facebook.com/ads/library/
2. Search competitor brand name, filter by country and ad category
3. Screenshot or describe active ads — format, hook, CTA, offer
4. Note how many active ads and approximate launch dates

## Output format
1. **Competitor creative inventory** — active ad count, format breakdown, messaging themes per competitor
2. **CTA and offer analysis** — what action competitors are pushing, what offers they're promoting
3. **Rotation frequency** — how often competitors refresh creative (signals budget/testing velocity)
4. **Messaging gaps** — angles no competitor is using that you can own
5. **Patterns to test vs patterns to break** — industry conventions worth following or deliberately avoiding
6. **Timeline flags** — recent ad volume spikes suggesting new campaign pushes

## Practical example
Three main competitors each running 15-25 active Meta ads. All three lead with feature-comparison messaging and "book a demo" CTAs. None use customer testimonials as primary creative, none run educational content ads, only one uses video. Recommendation: test UGC testimonial ads with a free trial CTA — a combination no competitor is using.

## When to use it
- Quarterly competitive reviews to track messaging shifts
- Before creative brainstorms to identify white space in the market
- When entering a new market and need to understand the creative landscape fast
- When CPA rises and you suspect competitors are getting more aggressive
